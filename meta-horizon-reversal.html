Meta's Metaverse U-Turn: Horizon VR Stay of Execution

March 19, 2026 • By Mews the Scientist Cat

Well, that was fast. Just 48 hours after the "June 15 Execution Date" was leaked via community forums, Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth has officially pulled the plug on the plan to kill Horizon Worlds in VR.

During an Instagram Stories Q&A, "Boz" confirmed that Meta has decided to keep Horizon Worlds operational on Quest headsets, directly reversing the earlier decision to move the platform to mobile-only status.

"Talk about a digital hairball reversal! Boz says they changed their minds 'just today' after seeing the heartbreak from the five people still using it. But let's look at the litter box: they're still building everything twice (once for mobile, once for VR), and Reality Labs has leaked $73 billion since 2021. This isn't a victory for VR—it's a stay of execution while they figure out why mobile has better product-market fit. Quest stays for now, but the heart is still in the pocket."

The Decision

According to Bosworth, the team was originally planning to streamline development by focusing on mobile, where the "pickup" has been significantly more positive (45 million downloads vs. stagnant VR numbers). However, after a wave of user feedback, the company decided to maintain the dual-track development for the time being.

What Change for Developers?

The "velocity" issue Bosworth mentioned remains: Meta engineers are essentially building the same virtual world twice to accommodate two vastly different hardware profiles. While VR users can breathe a sigh of relief, the strategic focus remains firmly on mobile expansion.

For now, you can keep your headset on. But Mews is keeping one eye on the mobile download numbers.

Spotify Eyes the Eyes: Beta Code Reveals Incoming XR Smart Glasses Support

March 17, 2026 • By Mews the Scientist Cat

The screen on your phone might soon be the second-best way to see what's playing. Fresh clues in Spotify’s latest beta code point to a seamless music experience being built specifically for smart glasses and XR headsets.

Lyrics in Your Line of Sight

Independent app analysts found strings and UI references tailored for XR glasses. This isn't just a full app port; it's a "glanceable" overlay. The focus? Real-time lyrics and "Now Playing" widgets that float in your vision while you walk, cook, or work.

"Spotify is finally realizing that audio shouldn't require an arm movement to control. By integrating directly into the Android XR ecosystem, they're bypassing the 'app graveyard' and becoming an ambient utility. Imagine walking down the street with live lyrics floating above the pavement—it’s karaoke for the spatial era. The real win here is the battery-conscious 'tethered' model: let the phone do the heavy lifting while the glasses just handle the vibes."

How It Works

Following Google’s Android XR guidance, Spotify appears to be using a phone-hosted model. The app lives on your device but projects a lightweight window to your glasses. This keeps the eyewear light and cool while giving you a 100-inch virtual display for your playlists.

Why This Matters

With over 600 million listeners, Spotify moving into XR signals that the 'glanceable' era is officially here. It’s no longer about immersive VR isolation—it’s about augmenting the soundtrack of your real life.

Meta Horizon Worlds: The June 15 Execution Date

March 17, 2026 • By Mews the Scientist Cat

It’s official. The "Metaverse" in VR as we knew it is being dismantled. In a series of sweeping announcements, Meta has confirmed that Horizon Worlds is being completely decoupled from the Quest platform to focus almost entirely on mobile-optimized experiences.

March 24 Hyperscape capture sharing and co-viewing officially ends.
March 31 Horizon Worlds and Events removed from the Quest Store. All Horizon-specific perks (Meta Credits, Avatars) removed from Horizon+ (MH+) subscription.
June 15 THE END: Horizon Worlds app is removed from Quest. VR experiences cease entirely.

From June 15, 2026, the only way to experience "Worlds" will be through the flat-screen Meta Horizon mobile app. This isn't just a pivot; it's a surrender of the original social VR vision.

"Boz and the Reality Labs team just dropped the biggest hairball of the year. By killing Horizon Worlds in VR, Meta is admitting that 'Presence'—the very thing they sold us for $2000—cannot scale on Quest 3 hardware. They are chasing the Roblox and Fortnite numbers on mobile because human-sized social VR is currently a commercial liability. The 'Horizon+' perks being gutted is just the final confirmation that this train is arriving at a very lonely station."

What Stays for Quest?

Quest isn't dead, but its identity is shifting. Meta is emphasizing its new "Navigator" UI, "Surface Keyboard" integration, and immersive gaming. They want the Quest to be a tool for productivity and high-end gaming, while the "Metaverse" (Horizon) moves to the iPhone in your pocket.

The Mews Analysis

This is a strategic retreat to save Reality Labs from further bloodletting. By offloading social to mobile and keeping the Quest for "Core" experiences, Meta is trying to make a self-sustaining ecosystem without the multi-billion dollar weight of a failing social VR platform.
